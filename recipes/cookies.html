Cookies

Let's make some classic chocolate chip cookies!

This is your old-but-gold, traditional cookies to satisfy that need for sweets or that need for traditional baked goods. It is simple and easy — anyone can do it!

The recipe was taken from BBC's Good Food.

Ingrients

  • 150g salted butter, softened
  • 80g brown sugar
  • 80g granulated sugar
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 large egg
  • 225g plain flour
  • ½ tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 200g plain chocolate chips or chunks

Steps

  1. Heat the oven to 190°C and line two baking sheets with non-sticky baking paper.
  2. Put all of the butter, brown and granulated sugar into a bowl and beat until creamy.
  3. Beat in the vanilla extract and the egg.
  4. Sift the flour, bicarbonate and salt into the bowl and mix it with a wooden spoon (or sillicone spatula).
  5. Add the chocolate chunks and stir well.
  6. Use a tablespoon to make equal-sized balls of dough and space them apart in the tray.
  7. Bake for 8-10 minutes, until they are light brown on theesges and still slightly soft in the center.
  8. Let them cool off and they are ready!
